I need to clarify how YUV loses information.  For individual 24bit colors, the YUV <-> RGB conversion
is 1-1 over a somewhat limited range, due to 8-bit over/under flows, and, I think for some variants, 
clamping requirements due to YUV's origin in analog color TV signals.  

However, each 2x2 block of even origin contiguous pixel 
blocks, 24*4=96 bits of data, is coded with 3*8=24 bytes of YUV data.  

Differences in "luma" for pixels are captured with full spatial resolution, but pixels adjacent in 
a 2x2 block with the same luma might have different rgb combinations.

Note also the Bayer color sensor pattern in digital cameras has twice as many green sensors as either
reds or blues.  (I think the sensor values are the digital camera RAW files that higher end
cameras can save.  I don't know all they save for movies.)

The Y value is mostly green; here are two formula variants:

From https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/win32/medfound/about-yuv-video

new used for HDTV, ITU-R BT.709
Ypfla = 0.2125*R + 0.7154*G + 0.0721*B

Old std def TV, TU-R BT.601
YpflaOld = 0.299*R + 0.587*G + 0.114*B
